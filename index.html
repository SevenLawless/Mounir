About Mounir Faheem

Mounir Faheem was a celebrated Egyptian painter born in Rashid in 1935. Raised in Upper Egypt, his early years in Edfu and Aswan deeply influenced his art, infusing it with pharaonic symbols, Nubian culture, and the timeless beauty of the Nile.

A prodigious talent, he was drawing with precision by age six. After graduating from Cairo's Faculty of Fine Arts in 1962, he opened his own gallery in Alexandria and gained recognition through national and international exhibitions, from Cairo to Madrid.

Blending figurative style with cultural symbolism, Faheem created over 700 portraits. He passed away in 1983 in Alexandria, leaving behind a legacy rooted in Egyptian identity and artistic depth.

Key Dates – Mounir Faheem (1935–1983)

June 26, 1935
Born in Rashid (Rosetta), Beheira Governorate, Egypt.
1940s
Spent childhood in Edfu and Aswan, deeply influenced by Upper Egypt's culture and landscapes.
1949
Received primary certificate near Kom Ombo (north of Aswan).
Early 1950s
Attended high school in Beni Suef and earned his baccalaureate at Al-Nil AlAmiria High School.
1962
Graduated from the Faculty of Fine Arts, Cairo.
1965
Opened his own gallery in Alexandria.
1970
First exhibition in Cairo, covered by the press. Also participated in a prestigious group show at Palestine Hotel in Alexandria, attended by King Hussein of Jordan.
1970s
Exhibited at El Shams Club in Heliopolis; his work was acquired by the Egyptian Ministry of Tourism.
1970s–80s
Exhibited internationally (including in Madrid and Austria); produced over 700 portraits.
March 2, 1983
Passed away in his Alexandria apartment.
2019
His painting 'Two Nubian Girls' sold at Bonhams.

Legacy & Recognition

March 2019

Retrospective Exhibition at Cairo Museum

A major retrospective "Mounir Faheem: Egyptian Identity Through Art" was held at the Cairo Museum of Modern Egyptian Art, featuring over 50 pieces from his prolific career.

Read More
November 2019

"Two Nubian Girls" Sold at Bonhams

Faheem's iconic painting "Two Nubian Girls" achieved a record price at Bonhams London, highlighting the growing international recognition of his significant contribution to Egyptian art.

Read More
September 2021

New Biography Published

"The Art and Life of Mounir Faheem," a comprehensive biography authored by Dr. Nadia El-Sebai, explores the artist's influence on the Egyptian cultural renaissance of the mid-20th century.
